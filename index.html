#Our Story

2 Decades, One Heart: from Our Home to a Sanctuary

For over 20 years, our home has been more than just a house; it has been a hospital, a nursery, and a refuge. What began as a single rescue two decades ago turned into a lifelong mission to protect the voiceless cats and dogs of Ghana.

We didn't start as an organization; we started as two people who couldn't look away from an animal in need. For over 20 years, we have operated entirely from our own pockets—funding medical care, food, and shelter for hundreds of animals because we believe every life is special.

The Turning Point

While our hearts are limitless, our space is not. Having spent twenty years balancing our personal lives with the growing needs of Ghana's stray and abandoned population, we realized that to save more lives, we needed to build something bigger than ourselves. The need is urgent: Cats and dogs in our community often face extreme hardship, and there are far too few places for them to find permanent safety. We decided it was time to transition from a private rescue into a public sanctuary.

The Vision for the Future

The One&Only Animal Sanctuary is the culmination of twenty years of lessons, tears, and triumphs. We are building a permanent, sustainable facility designed to house:

  • 100+ cats in a safe, enriched environment where they can live with dignity.
  • Our 4 resident dogs who serve as the loyal guardians of our mission.

This sanctuary isn't just providing four walls and a roof; it's about providing a lifetime of peace. We are moving from "saving them in our home" to "building them a home of their own".

#Who We Are

WHO ARE WE?

Founder and Director: Fiorenza Krejza

She is an Italian/Ghanaian citizen who came to Ghana the first time in the 1994 and she fixed her residence there in the year 2003. As an animal lover, since she came, she started rescuing sick and injured cats from the road. Over of 20 years of hard work she hosted over hundreds of cats and dogs, only financing the huge costs of feeding and veterinary care by her own means. At the moment she hosts more than 50 cats and 3 dogs in her private house.

Co-founder: Magdalene Ayensu (Aba)

She was born in Anomabo (Central Region) and she studied at the Winneba University where she graduated in psychology. She started rescuing cats many years ago and at the moment she has 54 cats and one dog. Because the landlord didn't permit to keep her so many cats, Magdalene took a loan to build a temporary wooden cage where she moved the cats. She also sleeps there to avoid her cats to be stolen at night.

#Vision & Mission

Our Future & Purpose

VISION

Our Future: to lead a movement of compassion across Ghana where every domestic animal has a place to call home. We envision a future where our sanctuary serves as a beacon of animal welfare, providing a secure haven for over 100 residents and inspiring a nation to protect and cherish its feline and canine companions.

MISSION

Our Purpose: to provide a permanent sanctuary of safety, healing, and lifelong care for abandoned and vulnerable cats and dogs in Ghana. Rooted in over 20 years of private rescue experience, we are dedicated to transforming lives through expert rehabilitation, compassionate animal advocacy, and the development of a sustainable, purpose-built facility where every animal is treated as "The One and Only".

#Support Our Mission

Help Build the Permanent Sanctuary

Pet ownership is less common by Ghanaians. Only in the last years there was a trend change but mostly cats and dogs of the low-income citizens wander off the property. They are often semi-feral cats and dogs that live in the community by feeding on human garbage. They are not spayed and neutered and the overpopulation makes them at risk of poisoning and culling. This fuels an unregulated and inhumane meat trade. There are no shelters that can provide rescue and permanent housing.

For 20 years, we have funded every rescue ourselves. Now, we plan to build a sanctuary and we need your help to lay the foundation for a future where every animal is safe. Here is how you can help us reach our goal of a permanent home for more than 100 cats and 4 dogs.

Financial Contribution

Direct donations go straight toward construction materials, veterinary bills, and daily nutrition.

  • One-Time Gift: every cedi/dollar/euro helps us to buy a brick or a bag of life-saving kibble.
  • Monthly Sponsorship: become a "sanctuary guardian" by providing consistent monthly support that ensures our residents never go hungry.
  • The "Brick by Brick" Fund: specifically for the construction of our new facility.
Supply Donation

Maintaining a sanctuary requires more than just funds—we are always in need of physical supplies.

  • Food: high quality dry and wet food for cats and dogs.
  • Medical Supplies: flea/tick treatment, dewormers, and first-aid kits.
  • Comfort: blankets, towels, cat trees, and sturdy toys for our four dogs.
  • Cleaning Supplies: safe, non-toxic disinfectants to keep our environment healthy.
Sponsor a Resident

Don't have room at home? You can "adopt" one of our residents virtually!

How it works: choose a specific cat or one of our 4 dogs. Your monthly sponsorship covers their specific medical and dietary needs, and we'll send you regular updates on your "furry godchild".
